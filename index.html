About

Hello, my name is Nhung*. I am a computer science PhD student in Dr. Mika Rubinov's lab at Vanderbilt University. My research interests are interdisciplinary, lying at the intersection of neuroscience, genetics, and data science. My current project focuses on the genetic mechanisms that underpin functional connectivity in the human brain.

I earned a B.A. in computer science from Swarthmore College in 2019. I am a proud first-generation college graduate, and one of my lifelong goals is to work with educators on expanding access to both STEM education and higher education. Brain Genomics

The goal of this project is to elucidate the transcriptomic variance that underlies functional connectivity in human brain networks, ultimately to understand how functional disruptions caused by neuropsychiatric disorders are genetically instigated.

The ability to evaluate brain organization in the context of individual genetic variation has the potential to improve our clinical understanding of disorders such as major depression, schizophrenia, and Alzheimer's disease.

PRAGMA

PRAGMA is an interactive visual analytics tool for deriving individualized brain parcellations from population-based atlases, as a means for analyzing individual variability in functional brain organization.

News: PRAGMA has received Honorable Mention for Best Short Paper at the IEEE VIS 2020 conference!

Natural Selection Inference

We proposed a Hidden Markov Model to Convolutional Neural Network method that predicts natural selection strengths in genetic sequences.

This work was motivated by the historical lack of representation and diversity in human genetics studies. Our hope was to develop a data-driven approach that does not rely on prior domain knowledge, such that it can be applied to data from understudied human populations.
